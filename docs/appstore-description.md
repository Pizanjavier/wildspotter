### Description

Geographical radar for the overland community. Discovers wild overnight spots from raw geographic data. Instead of crowdsourced reviews that lead to overcrowded spots, WildSpotter analyzes raw geographic data across Spain to surface places no one has shared.

HOW IT WORKS

Every spot in WildSpotter has been processed through a 6-stage pipeline:

1. Radar — Scans OpenStreetMap data for dead-end tracks, dirt parkings, and clearings accessible by vehicle.
2. Terrain — Analyzes slope and elevation from satellite terrain data. Flat spots score higher.
3. Legal Check — Cross-references each spot against 4 official Spanish databases: Red Natura 2000, National Parks, Coastal Law (Ley de Costas), and Cadastre land classification.
4. Satellite AI — AI vision analyzes satellite imagery for ground surface quality, vehicle access, open space, and signs of van community.
5. Context — Evaluates surroundings: road noise, urban density, scenic value, privacy, and nearby amenities.
6. Score — Combines all data into a confidence score (0-100) so you find the best spots first.

FEATURES

- Interactive map with pre-processed spots across all of Spain
- Scan any area to discover nearby spots instantly
- Detailed score breakdown: terrain, AI, and context factors
- Legal status checklist per spot (Natura 2000, National Parks, Coastal Law, Cadastre)
- One-tap navigation to any spot via Google Maps
- Satellite inspection via Google Maps for manual validation
- Save favorite spots for quick access
- Offline cache — previously viewed areas work without connection
- Configurable filters: max slope, minimum score, hide restricted zones
- Legal zone overlay on the map (toggle in settings)
- Light and dark themes
- Available in English and Spanish

EARLY ACCESS

WildSpotter is currently in Early Access. All features are free and unlocked during the preview period. Your feedback helps shape the product.

DATA SOURCES

Spot data is derived from OpenStreetMap, terrain from Terrain-RGB tiles, legal boundaries from MITECO (Ministerio para la Transición Ecológica y el Reto Demográfico), and land classification from the Spanish Cadastre. Satellite analysis uses IGN Spain PNOA orthophotos.
