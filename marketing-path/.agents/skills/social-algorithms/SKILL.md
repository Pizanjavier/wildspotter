# Social Media Algorithms & Cross-Platform Strategy

## Instagram Algorithm (2025-2026)

### How Content Gets Ranked

Instagram runs separate algorithms for Feed, Reels, Stories, and Explore. There is no single "algorithm."

**Feed:** Ranks by (1) user engagement history with your content, (2) post engagement velocity, (3) creator credibility, (4) relationship strength. Top signals: time spent on post > comments > likes > shares > profile taps.

**Reels (primary growth channel):**
- **#1 signal: DM shares** — the strongest signal for reaching new audiences. Content people send to friends gets exponentially more distribution
- **#2: Watch completion** — how much of the Reel viewers watch. The 3-second mark is critical; drop-off before 3s kills distribution
- **#3: Saves** — indicates high-value content worth revisiting
- **#4: Comments** — especially replies and conversations
- **#5: Story shares** — when people reshare your Reel to their Story
- **#6: Likes** — weakest signal, but still counts
- **"Sends Per Reach"** is the new north-star metric, replacing engagement rate

**Stories:** Designed for close connections, not growth. Key signals: viewing frequency, DM replies, sticker interactions. Use for community warmth, not reach.

**Explore:** Ranks by post popularity speed (how fast engagement accumulates), user's past Explore behavior, and creator engagement consistency.

### Key Thresholds & Numbers

- 94% of Instagram distribution comes from AI recommendations — the algorithm decides, not your follower count
- Reels engagement rate: ~2.46% (vs 0.70% for static images) — Reels are the growth format
- Original content gets 40-60% more distribution than reposts
- 10+ reposts in 30 days = excluded from recommendations entirely
- ~50% of users watch Reels muted — strong visual hooks and text overlays are mandatory
- Carousels now support up to 20 slides
- Adding audio to carousels/photos enables distribution to the Reels feed

### What NOT to Do

- Don't dump multiple posts consecutively — triggers algorithmic suppression
- Don't use 30 hashtags — hashtag following was removed Dec 2024. Use 3-5 relevant ones
- Don't rely on hashtags for discovery — keywords in captions/bio are now the SEO mechanism
- Don't repost content from other platforms with watermarks (especially TikTok watermark on Reels)
- Account type (personal vs business) has zero impact on ranking

### Optimal Posting

- **Best times:** Thursday 9 AM, Wednesday 12 PM, Wednesday 6 PM (general benchmarks — adjust with Insights data)
- **Frequency:** 3-5 Reels/week + daily Stories. Space posts hours apart
- **Reels length:** 30-90 seconds for existing followers. Under 30 seconds for discovery/new audiences
- **Carousels:** Strong for saves and shares. Educational content performs best

### Community Tactics

- **5-3-1 Rule:** Daily, engage with 5 small accounts, 3 medium accounts, 1 large account in vanlife niche
- Interactive Story elements (polls, questions, quizzes) boost story tray placement
- Reply to every comment in the first hour — engagement velocity matters
- Craft captions that end with a question to drive comments
- Use Instagram Broadcast Channels for direct community updates

## TikTok Algorithm (2025-2026)

### How the FYP Works

TikTok tests every video in progressive waves. Phase 1: small audience (~200-500 views). If engagement exceeds threshold, Phase 2: larger pool. Repeat until engagement drops below threshold or video goes viral.

**Major 2025-2026 changes:**
- Videos now test with **followers first** before reaching non-followers — follower quality matters more than before
- Completion rate threshold for virality: **~70%** (up from ~50% in 2024)
- **Niche consistency** is heavily rewarded — posting across 3+ unrelated topics = ~45% lower reach. The algorithm builds "topical authority" per creator
- **AI-generated content is actively down-ranked** — TikTok prioritizes authentic human creators
- **Search is now a direct ranking metric** — TikTok SEO matters (keyword-rich captions, spoken keywords, text overlays)

### Ranking Signals

1. **Watch time & completion rate** (~40-50% of ranking weight) — a 15s video with 90% completion crushes a 60s video with 30% completion
2. **Shares and saves** — now outweigh likes (2025 shift toward deeper engagement)
3. **Rewatches** — signal strong interest
4. **Comments** — especially early comments
5. **Likes** — still count, weakest signal

### Key Thresholds

- 3-second hook rule: viewers must stay past 3 seconds or distribution dies
- 70% completion rate = threshold for expanded distribution
- TikTok "listens" to your video — spoken words and on-screen text are indexed for search
- The algorithm can identify and classify content topics from video alone

### Optimal Posting

- **Frequency:** 3-5x/week is the sweet spot. 1-2x/day if you can maintain quality
- **Video length:** 21-34 seconds gets highest engagement. 60+ seconds for monetization eligibility. 5-15 minutes had highest completion rates in TikTok's 2026 Long-Form Study (niche dependent)
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 per video, mix trending + niche-specific
- **Captions:** Keyword-rich, descriptive. TikTok SEO is real — treat captions like mini search optimization

### TikTok-Specific Tactics

- Use **Creator Search Insights** to find what your audience is actively searching for
- First comment strategy: pin a comment that sparks discussion
- Respond to comments with video replies — the algorithm loves this format
- Series/playlist feature: group related videos to build binge-watching loops
- Go live regularly to boost account authority
- Duet/Stitch trending content in your niche for discovery

### Trend-Riding Framework

1. Check TikTok's Trending page and Creator Search Insights daily
2. If a trend fits vanlife/travel/Spain niche → adapt within 24h
3. If it doesn't fit → skip. Niche consistency > trend-chasing
4. Use trending audio on Instagram Reels when it fits the mood
5. Never force a trend — authentic niche content outperforms forced trend participation

### Community Management Playbook

**Daily routine (30 min):**
1. Reply to ALL comments within 1 hour of posting (5 min)
2. Reply to DMs — especially "where is this?" questions (5 min)
3. 5-3-1 engagement: comment meaningfully on 5 small + 3 medium + 1 large vanlife account (15 min)
4. Check Stories mentions and reshare UGC (5 min)

**Comment response strategy:**
- "Where is this?" → "El radar te lo dirá 😏 [link in bio]" — never reveal coordinates publicly
- Skeptical comments → engage genuinely, explain the tech without being defensive
- Competitor mentions → "Nosotros no compartimos spots — los descubrimos con datos" — reframe, don't attack
- Negative feedback → acknowledge, don't delete unless spam/hate
- Feature requests → "Apuntado 📝" and actually note it

**Crisis playbook:**
- If accused of promoting illegal camping → "WildSpotter muestra datos legales (Natura 2000, Costas, Catastro) para que TÚ decidas. No promovemos nada ilegal."
- If a spot gets overcrowded after going viral → consider removing specific location data from the post, acknowledge the tension
